# Reporting and Stakeholder Communication

## Introduction
Reporting is one of the most important phases of penetration testing. It ensures that identified vulnerabilities are communicated clearly to stakeholders.

---

## Report Structure

### Executive Summary
A high-level overview of findings for non-technical stakeholders.

### Technical Findings
Detailed explanation of vulnerabilities including:
- Description
- Impact
- Proof of Concept

### Risk Analysis
Assign severity levels based on impact and likelihood.

### Remediation
Provide actionable solutions to fix vulnerabilities.

---

## Audience Tailoring
Reports should be customized based on the audience:
- Managers need simplified summaries
- Developers need technical details

---

## Metrics and KPIs
Common metrics include:
- Number of vulnerabilities
- Severity levels
- Time to remediation
- Exploit success rate

---

## Objectives
- Communicate findings clearly
- Provide actionable recommendations
- Help organizations improve security posture

---

## Conclusion
Effective reporting bridges the gap between technical findings and business impact, ensuring vulnerabilities are properly addressed.
